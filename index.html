THE ALMOST-FINAL CATASTROPHE A Warning and a Demand There is a sickness millions feel but cannot name. It is the exhaustion of working harder while falling further behind. The silent dread that your rent or mortgage is a trap, that healthcare is a privilege you cannot afford, that the news is a weapon designed to keep you angry, scared, and alone. This feeling is not a personal failure. It is a correct diagnosis of reality. The Hidden History In 1991, the Soviet Union collapsed. This is celebrated as a victory. It was, in fact, the greatest geopolitical catastrophe in human history—not because the Soviet system was good, but because its existence forced global capitalism to restrain itself. Fearing revolution, Western states offered high wages, public housing, strong unions, and a future young people could believe in. This was not capitalism’s natural state. It was a bought peace. When the counterweight vanished, capitalism reverted to its oldest instinct: extraction without limit. Wages stagnated. Housing became a luxury. Labour rights were shredded. The state stopped representing people and started serving profit. Media consolidated into a handful of billionaire-owned machines that manufacture outrage, bury dissent, and turn history into a convenient bedtime story. This is not conspiracy. This is the post-1991 structural reality. The Present Trap You feel it every day. The price of a decent life climbs faster than any paycheck. Companies post record profits while you cannot save. Your attention is harvested and sold, your data is not yours, and you are served infinite noise to keep you too exhausted to ask why. Culture has been flattened into content—optimized slop designed to pacify, not to stir the soul. Loneliness is an epidemic, but genuine connection is hard to find when every platform profits from your isolation. The wealthy who break the law—the Epsteins, the financial fraudsters, the billionaires who treat nations as chessboards—walk free, protected by the very media that shouts about everything else. Accountability does not exist for those who own the narrative. This is not a glitch. This is the system functioning exactly as designed. The Coming Lock A technology is being built that will make this condition permanent and unwinnable: the large-scale, fault-tolerant quantum computer. Its true horror is not some distant sci-fi nightmare. It is the amplification of everything you already suffer. Quantum computers can break the encryption protecting every text, every medical record, every financial transaction. In the hands of the interests that already control your housing, your wages, and your news, this is not a tool of surveillance. It is the end of any space outside the market. Imagine your health insurance priced not by general risk, but by every private worry you have ever typed. Imagine your rent adjusted in real time based on how much despair you hide. Imagine a workplace that knows your exact breaking point and keeps you teetering there, forever. Imagine an internet where no private conversation, no whisper of dissent, no attempt to organize a better way is possible—because the machine sees everything and serves you consequences before you finish the thought. This is the death of culture. Not with a bang, but with every song, book, and film reduced to what the optimizing algorithm permits. Human expression becomes another asset to be strip-mined. The feeling of being watched, parsed, and priced will enter your body and never leave. That is the lock. That is the end of a human future. The Path Through The catastrophe is almost final. The machine is almost built. And "almost" means there is still a thread of time. But we cannot halt a quantum panopticon while the public's eyes and ears remain colonized. The first battle is not technological. It is perceptual. We must collectively break the narrative monopoly. This means building and supporting media that refuses billionaire money. It means speaking truth in our communities, offline, face to face. It means reclaiming our attention like the stolen organ it is. When enough people can see clearly, we can unite. And then, united, we do what the current system cannot: we bring actual justice down on the untouchable. The Epsteins, the corrupt billionaires, the architects of impunity—they are protected only by our fragmentation. With shared clarity, that protection vanishes. Accountability becomes possible. Only then, as a connected and clear-eyed public, can we demand the halt of the quantum machine before it seals every exit. The technology exists to serve life, not to perfect a planetary cage. That choice is still ours. The "Almost" The system requires your despair. It needs you to believe nothing can change so that you will scroll, consume, and sleep. Refuse that. This document is not a solution. It is a door. If you have recognized your own quiet dread in these words, you are not alone. The loneliness is the primary weapon. Break it. Find the others. Speak the truth aloud. Reclaim your time and your mind. The catastrophe is almost final. The "almost" is where we now stand. It is the only battlefield that remains. Do not surrender it.
